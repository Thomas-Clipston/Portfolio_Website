Professional Growth Goals

Year 2 Focus · Reflection · Professional Identity

As I move into my second year of teacher’s college, I am continuing to develop my professional identity through intentional goal setting and reflection. These goals reflect areas where I aim to grow as an educator, while aligning my practice with the Ontario College of Teachers (OCT) Standards.

1. Building Inclusive and Meaningful Relationships

I aim to foster stronger, more inclusive relationships with my students by creating a classroom environment where all learners feel valued, respected, and supported. I recognize that strong relationships are foundational to student success, engagement, and well-being. Moving forward, I will focus on learning more about my students’ identities, interests, and learning needs, while adapting my teaching practices to reflect this understanding. I will also work to create a classroom culture that promotes trust, collaboration, and a sense of belonging.

Connection to OCT Standards:

This goal connects directly to the Commitment to Students and Student Learning standard, as it emphasizes supporting the well-being and development of all students. It also aligns with Professional Practice, as I work to create inclusive and responsive learning environments that reflect the diverse needs of learners.
2. Strengthening Collaboration with Peers and Experts

I aim to improve my teaching practice by collaborating more frequently with colleagues, mentor teachers, and other experts in the subjects I teach. By engaging in professional dialogue and seeking feedback, I can gain new perspectives and refine my instructional strategies. This goal involves actively seeking opportunities to co-plan lessons, discuss assessment strategies, and reflect on student learning with others. Through collaboration, I hope to strengthen both my content knowledge and my ability to deliver effective, engaging instruction.

Connection to OCT Standards:

This goal aligns with the Professional Learning standard, as it reflects my commitment to ongoing growth through collaboration and reflection. It also connects to Leadership in Learning Communities, as working with peers contributes to a shared culture of professional learning and continuous improvement.
